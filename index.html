Malaysia Aviation

Overview

This dashboard presents key insights into the aviation landscape in malaysia, including flight routes, airport counts, and the longest direct flights from Kuala Lumpur International Airport (KLIA). The visualisation is structured into different sections, providing a comprehensive view of the country's aviation network.

Explore The World: Longest Direct Flight From KLIA

The dot map with routes shows the top five longest direct flights departing from KLIA.

London Heathrow (LHR): 10605km

Charles de Gaulle Airport (CDG): 10442km

Amsterdam Airport Schiphol (AMS): 10237km

Frankfurt Airport (FRA): 9998km

Auckland Airport (AKL): 8703km

Country's Airport Count

The bar chart compares the number of airports in different countries. The United States tops the list with 1,512 airports, followed by Canada, Austria, and others. Malaysia is ranked 41st globally, with a total of 40 airports.

Airports' Connectivity in Malaysia

This proportional symbol map showcases the connectivity between airports within Malaysia. The size of each circle represents the number of domestic routes available from the airport, with larger circles indicating more extensive connectivity.

Lines between the airports highlight the flight routes, with bolder lines representing airports with more extensive domestic routes.

The key airports featured include:

Kuala Lumpur International Airport (KLIA): The largest and most connected airport.

Kuching International Airport (KCH), Penang International Airport (PEN), and Kota Kinabalu International Airport (BKI) also show notable connectivity.

Malaysia's Airport Type

This radial plot shows the number of airports in Malaysia based on their type. The majority of the airports are small airports, followed by medium airports and heliports. There is only one large airport in Malaysia, which is KLIA.

Monthly International Passenger Traffic Heat Map in Malaysia

This heat map shows the monthly passenger traffic in Malaysia. Each cell represents the rank of the month based on the number of passengers in each year. The darker the cell, the higher the rank. We can observe that there are three peaks in the heat map.
The first peak is in March, followed by July to August, and the last peak is in December. These peaks are due to the school holidays and festive seasons. There is a significant drop after February of 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. The international passenger traffic slowly recovers after the lockdown (see 2020).
